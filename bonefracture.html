BONE FRACTURE

Please note that this treatment is for instant medication to stop damages. If you have major injuries consult a certified doctor

Bone fractures are caused by accidents and it takes more time to recover if we don't give better treatment. It will not recover properly.

HERE'S A STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE, PLEASE FOLLOW THESE STEPS

STEP-1

IDENTIFY THE BROKEN PART

Identification involves examining the affected area through pressing the broken area carefully and delicately. This is crucial for determining the appropriate treatment and facilitating the healing process.

STEP-2

ATTACH TWO STRAIGHT RODS OR STICKS AND COVER IT TIGHTLY WITH BANDAGES LIKE ADHESIVE BANDAGES.

Create a sturdy structure by fastening two straight rods together, then tightly wrap them with adhesive bandages for added reinforcement and stability. This innovative technique ensures a secure and durable bond, ideal for various practical applications.

IF IT IS VERY SERIOUS, PLEASE CONSULT A DOCTOR.

FINGER BONE FRACTURE

ATTACH AND COVER TWO FINGERS WITH BANDAGE OR PLASTER

Wrap the bandage snugly around the injured fingers, ensuring proper support and protection. Then, secure with adhesive plaster to keep it in place during movement.
