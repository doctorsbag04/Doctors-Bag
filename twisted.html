HANDS & LEGS TWISTED

Please note that this treatment is for instant medication to stop damages. If you have major injuries consult a certified doctor

Twisting commonly results in sprains, involves the overstretching or tearing of ligaments. Immediate treatment is required.

HERE'S A STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE, PLEASE FOLLOW THESE STEPS

STEP-1

MASSAGE ON TWISTED PARTS SLOWLY

Massaging a twisted part should be done with care and patience to promote healing and alleviate discomfort. Begin by gently warming the area with your hands to increase blood flow and prepare the muscles. Use slow circular motions and apply light pressure with your fingertips, gradually increasing it as tolerated.

STEP-2

APPLY ICE BAG OR ICE CUBES ON THE TWISTED PART SLOWLY

Applying ice to the twisted part helps to reduce swelling and alleviate pain. To do this, take an ice bag or a few ice cubes wrapped in a thin cloth or towel. Gently place the ice on the affected area, moving it slowly in a circular motion. Make sure not to apply the ice directly to the skin to prevent frostbite. Continue this process for about 15-20 minutes. Repeat this every 2-3 hours during the first 48 hours after the injury.

STEP-3

COVER THE TWISTED PART TIGHTLY WITH ADHESIVE BANDAGES

Make sure that bandages are snug but not so tight as to restrict circulation. The bandages should fully cover the twisted part and extend slightly beyond its edges to provide a secure seal. Regularly check the bandage to ensure it remains in place and replace it if it becomes loose or soiled.
